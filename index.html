Understanding the global burden of rheumatic heart disease is critically important and technically challenging. Estimating how many people live with RHD (prevalence), develop RF and RHD (incidence) and die from RHD and itsâ€™ complications (mortality) helps to understand public health priorities, resource needs and opportunities for intervention. The atlas provides burden of disease estimates based on 3 methodologies. For technical details about each of these methods, click on the heading title.


1. Global Burden of Disease Study

Uses global data to calculate estimates for the burden of RHD in every country.

2. Echo screening studies

A modern approach uses heart ultrasound (echocardiography) to screen young people for RHD and calculate an estimated population prevalence.

3. Auscultation studies

An older approach to use stethoscopes to screen young people for RHD and calculate a population prevalence.
